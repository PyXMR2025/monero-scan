# You are using leveldb badly
author: Kingdud
created_at: 2026-02-05T04:06:01Z
status: closed

I wrote about this issue (in very unkind terms, I admit) to the Ethereum team, who also use LevelDB. My unkindness to them was, well, let's just say I'm not pointing that anger and frustration at you, rather, warning you of what you'll find if you go looking. Anyway, here's the short version:

1. Every time you write to leveldb and call sync to commit that write, it doesn't just append the new data to the DB. It rewrites the the entire table/section of the DB that was changed.
2. Because of point 1, calling sync constantly means you are limiting yourself to storage write performance. Using defaults, I was seeing a sustained 1.4GB/s of writes to my NVMe SSD during initial syncing. Despite it only ingesting < 1000 blocks during that time and the DB only being 20GB in total size. (translation: it was writing 100s of GB to a DB that was only 20GB in total size)
3. Setting db-sync-mode to fastest:async:100000blocks still leaves significant write pressure on storage (far exceeding new data) to the tune of 600-800MB/s for roughly 20-30MB/s of actually new data.
4. This means that to sync a 100GB blockchain, the storage device ends up doing something on the order of 20TB of writes.

There are various ways to fix it. The best is to discard levelDB entirely, but I understand the massive technical debt that incurs. The next best thing is to call sync() far less frequently and redesign your database to have checkpoints where you can know that data up to X is good, but data after X is suspect. Essentially what a normal (Oracle, MySQL, etc) DB transaction log does.
